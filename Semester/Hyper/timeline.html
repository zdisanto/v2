Timeline


Oswego City Fire Department History

Origin

1678

First fire engine company goes into service in Boston, Massachusetts



1743

Thomas Lote designs the first fire engine made in America

1800s

OFD Duty Uniforms early 1800s

1800s

Oswego Fire dept 1800's Steamer, hose wagon and hook and ladder.Station 3 on West Bridge st. before West 2nd St station.

1821


First rubber lined cotton web fire hose was patented by J.Boyd of Boston


1841

America's first self-propelled and steam-powered fire engine was built in New York. This machine was not adopted as it was dangerous and unreliable.

1853

A photo of a burning mill in Oswego by George Barnard. This is one of the earliest news photos ever taken. George Barnard was also an official photographer for William Tecumseh Sherman during the Civil war.

1857

The Oswego Starch Factory became the largest starch factory of its kind in the world. The factory had its own fire department, which was established in 1857. The Kingsford Fire Pump wagon was hand-drawn and used a soda and acid combination to extinguish fires.The Kingsford fire pump wagon was first patented in 1867 and built in 1869 at the Kingsford Foundry & Machine Works in Oswego. Known as the "Little Giant," the fire wagon is a part of the historical society collections and has been placed on long-term loan to the firefighters museum.

1876

The City Of Oswego Fire Department is officially founded



1878

The fire escape ladder is patented by Joseph Winters

1904

The Utica Saturday Globe Feb 13, 1904, "The plant of the Oswego Starch Factory, controlled by the Corn Products Company, otherwise known as the starch trust, was burned Wednesday. The fire started in the chemical room about 4 o'clock in the afternoon, and before anything could be done the building was a mass of flames. Although the entire Fire Department was on the scene within a few minutes, no human power could have stopped the spread of the flames and building after building caught and went up in smoke. The factory was running full-handed, and about 700 employees will be thrown out of work. It was one of the most disastrous fires that has visited Oswego in many a day. The property was valued at about $500,000, and occupied one of the best manufacturing sites in the northern part of the State, covering several acres."


1905

The fire modern fire engine is mounted on a truck body and was constructed by Knox Automobile of Springfield, Massachusetts.

1930


With fire fighters in need of a way to reach the upper floors of American buildings that were increasing in height, ladders as tall as 150 feet began appearing on fire trucks.


1940

Fire trucks began to be fitted with aerial platforms—known as “cherry pickers”—that allow fire fighters to ascend to formerly unreachable corners of any building.

1953

Oswego Engine 3 back in 1953 when it was out for delivery from Mack


1960

Modern day fire truck is born. These are equipped with water pumps, ladders, cherry-pickers and enclosed seats for the crew.

1969

First Residential Smoke Alarm released

1979

Iconic Oswego Candy Co is on fire

1985

New Oswego Fire Department East Side Facility is being built for $1.2 million


1985

First plastic head fire extinguisher line is developed

1993

First battery operated carbon monoxide alarm is released


1995

First 10 year sealed battery smoke alarm released
